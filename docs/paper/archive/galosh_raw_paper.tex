% ----------------------------------------------------------------- Abstract
\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{GALOSH} (Generalized Anscombe LOcal SHrinkage), a fully
\emph{blind}, \emph{training-free} denoiser that operates directly on the raw
Bayer mosaic. GALOSH estimates the per-image Poisson--Gaussian noise model from
the noisy frame alone, variance-stabilizes it with a generalized Anscombe
transform, and removes noise with a single forward pass of local shrinkage on a
stride-1 Walsh--Hadamard luma/chroma decomposition followed by a luminance-guided
chroma reconstruction. Unlike learned denoisers it requires no clean/noisy pairs
and no training, and unlike block-matching methods its computation is
data-independent and local. On two real-noise raw benchmarks --- SIDD Medium
(80 full-resolution images) and RawNIND (1493 cross-camera crops) --- GALOSH is
the best \emph{blind, training-free} method on perceptual quality (LPIPS/DISTS)
while using the \emph{lowest} per-pixel compute of any method compared
($3.4\text{k}$ MAC/pixel, $3.9$--$6.4\times$ below classical BM3D/NLM and
$18$--$360\times$ below learned baselines). The same algorithm has a bit-exact
INT16 fixed-point realization: data-independent, line-buffered, and
frame-buffer-free, it maps onto a modest ISP MAC array
($0.5$--$1$~TMAC/s) at real-time 1080p/4K by an operation-count model, and the
FP32 GPU pipeline already denoises 1080p at 55\,fps. GALOSH thus closes the gap
between offline raw denoising quality and on-sensor, real-time deployability.
\end{abstract}

% ----------------------------------------------------------------- Intro
\section{Introduction}
Denoising the raw sensor mosaic --- before demosaicing and color processing ---
is where a camera pipeline has the most signal to recover, because the noise is
still close to its physical Poisson--Gaussian form and has not been correlated by
downstream stages. Two families dominate. \emph{Classical} estimators
(BM3D~\cite{bm3d}, non-local means~\cite{nlm}) are training-free but assume a
known noise level and, in the case of BM3D, rely on \emph{content-dependent}
block matching that is ill-suited to a fixed-latency hardware stage. \emph{Learned}
denoisers (self-supervised ones such as Blind2Unblind~\cite{b2u} and
Neighbor2Neighbor, or supervised CNNs) reach excellent quality but need training
data, are tied to the sensors/noise they were trained on, and carry a compute
budget (tens of thousands of MACs/pixel, multi-scale feature buffers) that does
not fit an image-signal-processor (ISP) streaming stage.

We argue that a practical on-sensor denoiser must be simultaneously
(i)~\emph{blind} (estimate the noise from the frame itself, no oracle),
(ii)~\emph{training-free} (no data, generalizes across sensors),
(iii)~\emph{perceptually strong} (judged by LPIPS/DISTS, not only PSNR), and
(iv)~\emph{ISP-deployable} (low, data-independent compute; line buffers; no frame
buffer). GALOSH is designed for all four at once. Its contributions are:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item A blind, training-free raw-Bayer pipeline that estimates the
Poisson--Gaussian model per image and denoises in a single local-shrinkage pass
(Sec.~\ref{sec:method}).
\item State-of-the-art perceptual quality \emph{among blind, training-free
methods} on SIDD Medium and RawNIND, at the lowest per-pixel compute of any
method compared (Sec.~\ref{sec:results}).
\item A bit-exact INT16 fixed-point realization and an operation-count ISP
throughput model showing real-time 1080p/4K on a modest fixed-function array,
backed by a working streaming GPU implementation (Sec.~\ref{sec:int16}).
\end{itemize}

% ----------------------------------------------------------------- Method
\section{Method}\label{sec:method}
GALOSH processes the Bayer frame in a single forward pass of eleven phases
(P0--P10); all parameters are either derived from the noise model or fixed
constants (no per-image tuning).

\paragraph{P0 --- Blind noise estimation.}
We fit a Poisson--Gaussian model $\operatorname{Var}[x]=\alpha\,\mathbb{E}[x]+
\sigma^2$ from the noisy frame alone by a robust lower-envelope regression over
local mean/variance bins (a Foi--Alenius-style estimator), giving the per-image
$(\alpha,\sigma^2)$ with no oracle.

\paragraph{P1 --- Variance stabilization (GAT).}
A generalized Anscombe transform $f(x)=\tfrac{2}{\alpha}\sqrt{\alpha x+\tfrac38
\alpha^2+\sigma^2}$ maps the signal-dependent noise to approximately unit
variance, so a single global shrinkage threshold is valid everywhere. Per-CFA-channel
MAD gives a residual scale used to normalize the stabilized frame.

\paragraph{P2 --- Achromatic dark reference.}
A CFA-aware iteratively-reweighted (IRLS) estimate of the local achromatic floor
is subtracted, preventing the chroma channels from absorbing luminance shading.

\paragraph{P3--P4 --- Luma/chroma decomposition.}
A \emph{stride-1, cycle-spun} $2{\times}2$ Walsh--Hadamard transform turns each
Bayer quad into one luma plane $L$ and three chroma planes $C_1,C_2,C_3$. Because
the CFA samples chroma at half the luma rate, the chroma planes are kept at
half resolution (band-limit-faithful, no information lost).

\paragraph{P5 --- Local shrinkage.}
Luma is denoised at full resolution by cycle-spun local shrinkage (LOSH) with an
MAD-driven threshold. Chroma is denoised at half resolution by a luminance-guided
LOESS (a degree-1, $Y$-guided local linear regression --- a guided filter), built
as a 3-level scale pyramid so a single strength control walks smoothly from
detail-preserving to flat-region-smoothing behavior.

\paragraph{P6--P7 --- Chroma reconstruction.}
The denoised half-resolution chroma is upsampled to full resolution by a
luminance-guided joint-bilateral EWA Jinc-Lanczos-3 (``K16'') kernel: the jinc
interpolation is modulated by a bilateral weight on the full-resolution luma, so
chroma edges snap to luma edges via a cross-channel structural prior.

\paragraph{Chroma-range robustness.}
Both the guided-LOESS regression and the jinc upsampling are \emph{unbounded}
operators --- a degree-1 regression extrapolates at edges, and the jinc kernel
has negative side lobes that can ring past the local data range. Left unchecked,
the resulting chroma over-shoot is amplified by the white-balance gain (notably
the blue channel, $\sim\!2.7\times$) into isolated magenta speckles at high-contrast
luma edges. We therefore clamp every reconstructed chroma value to the
\emph{local input chroma range} (``the denoiser may not invent color the input
lacks''). This touches $\sim\!1.4\%$ of pixels, is identical across all four
numeric precisions, and improves aggregate perceptual quality (Sec.~\ref{sec:results}).

\paragraph{P8--P10 --- Inversion.}
A per-pixel inverse WHT recombines $L,C_1,C_2,C_3$, the dark reference is restored,
and the inverse GAT returns to the linear raw domain. The output is a denoised
Bayer frame, ready for any demosaic/ISP back-end.

% ----------------------------------------------------------------- Experiments
\section{Experimental Setup}\label{sec:exp}
\paragraph{Datasets.} \textbf{SIDD Medium}~\cite{sidd} --- 80 full-resolution
($\sim$16\,MP) real-noise smartphone Bayer images. \textbf{RawNIND}~\cite{rawnind}
--- 1493 cross-camera $512{\times}512$ real-noise crops spanning many sensors and
ISOs.

\paragraph{Protocol.} All methods are run \emph{blind} (no clean/noisy pairs, no
per-image noise oracle) and \emph{training-free}, except Blind2Unblind which is a
trained self-supervised network shown only as an upper reference. Fidelity
(PSNR/SSIM) is measured on the raw Bayer output; perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS,
NIQE) are computed on a calibrated sRGB render (per-image white-balance and color
matrix from the DNG metadata; edge-aware demosaic). We additionally report
ablations that share a variance-stabilizing front-end (VST\,+\,method), isolating
the contribution of GALOSH's native blind estimation.

\paragraph{Methods.} GALOSH (FP32 reference, and the bit-exact INT16 fixed-point
variant); classical BM3D-CFA and NLM-CFA with native MAD noise estimation; their
VST front-end ablations; and Blind2Unblind as a trained upper bound.

\section{Results}\label{sec:results}
Tables~\ref{tab:sidd_medium} and~\ref{tab:rawnind} report the two benchmarks.
Among blind, training-free methods GALOSH is best on the perceptual metrics
(LPIPS, DISTS) on both datasets, and competitive on PSNR/SSIM, while the classical
baselines --- even with a shared VST front-end --- trail on perceptual quality by
a wide margin (LPIPS $\sim\!0.27$--$0.43$ vs.\ GALOSH $\sim\!0.20$--$0.24$). GALOSH
narrows much of the gap to the \emph{trained} Blind2Unblind without any training.

\section{INT16 Fixed-Point and ISP Suitability}\label{sec:int16}
GALOSH has a second realization in pure INT16 fixed-point that is
\emph{bit-exact} between a CPU reference and a GPU streaming implementation. Its
purpose is on-sensor deployability, which GPU/CPU timings do \emph{not} measure:
those hosts are FP-optimized and merely \emph{emulate} a fixed-point datapath in
software, whereas a real ISP datapath is natively fixed-point. The
host-independent metric is the \textbf{operation count per pixel}.

\paragraph{Compute.} Instrumented over the full P0--P10 pipeline, GALOSH costs
$\approx\!3380$ multiply-accumulates and $\approx\!137$ LUT/special-function
operations per pixel, and these counts are \emph{resolution-independent}
($3390.9$ at 1080p vs.\ $3370.3$ at 4K), i.e.\ data-independent streaming. An ISP
MAC-array model $\text{fps}=(\text{MAC\_rate}/3380)/\text{pixels}$ then shows a
$0.5$~TMAC/s block (very modest) reaches real-time 1080p (73\,fps) and a
$1$~TMAC/s block reaches real-time 4K (36\,fps).

\paragraph{Memory.} GALOSH is row-streaming: the only on-chip state is the line
buffers sized by the largest vertical kernel (WHT $8{\times}8$, LOESS 15-tap, K16
$5{\times}5$), $\sim\!200$\,KB at 1080p in INT16 --- $20$--$40\times$ less than a
full INT16 frame, and crucially with \emph{no} frame buffer.

\paragraph{Why GALOSH and not the baselines.} Table~\ref{tab:isp} compares
per-pixel compute and, more fundamentally, streamability. GALOSH is the lightest
($3.9$--$6.4\times$ below BM3D/NLM, $18$--$360\times$ below the learned nets) and
is data-independent with local access. BM3D's collaborative filtering decides
\emph{which} blocks to group from content at run time, so it cannot be a
fixed-latency streaming stage at all --- a limitation its MAC count alone hides.
The learned nets need multi-scale feature buffers, not line buffers. GALOSH is
thus uniquely light $\times$ data-independent-streamable $\times$ best-perceptual,
and the GPU INT16 port is a \emph{working} streaming fixed-point implementation
(streamability demonstrated, not asserted).

% ----------------------------------------------------------------- Conclusion
\section{Conclusion}
GALOSH shows that blind, training-free raw denoising need not trade away either
perceptual quality or hardware deployability. A per-image Poisson--Gaussian fit, a
variance-stabilized Walsh--Hadamard luma/chroma shrinkage, and a luminance-guided
chroma reconstruction give the best perceptual quality among blind, training-free
methods on two real-noise raw benchmarks --- at the lowest per-pixel compute of any
method compared, in a data-independent, line-buffered, frame-buffer-free pipeline
that a modest ISP MAC array runs in real time. The same variance-stabilized
local-shrinkage core extends naturally beyond the raw mosaic to already-processed
YUV/video frames; that domain, with its distinct temporal baselines, is the
subject of a separate study. A cycle-accurate HLS/FPGA synthesis is the natural
next step toward silicon.
